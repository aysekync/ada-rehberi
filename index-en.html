How to Rent a Car in Cyprus?

Get ready to explore the island freely in just 4 simple steps.

📄
01

Prepare Your Documents

To rent a car in Cyprus, your national driving license (TR or international) and your passport/ID are sufficient. No extra international driving license is required.

🔞
02

Pay Attention to Age Limits

To rent a car, a minimum age of 21 years and 2 years of driving experience are usually required. For luxury segment vehicles, this limit may increase to 25.

✈️
03

Choose Airport Delivery

To save time, book your rental car with Ercan Airport delivery. Company representatives will meet you at the exit with a sign showing your name.

🛡️
04

Check the Insurance

Rental cars in Cyprus come with excess or Collision Damage Waiver (CDW). Be sure to ask about tire, windshield, and headlight coverage before signing the contract.

📌 Important Reminder: Rental cars in Cyprus have Red license plates, and traffic flows on the left, not the right!

Safe Driving Guide in Cyprus

⬅️ Traffic Flows on the Left

In Cyprus, steering is on the right, and traffic flows on the left. Remember to yield to vehicles coming from your right when entering roundabouts.

📸 Fixed Speed Cameras

There are numerous fixed speed cameras across the island. Traffic fines are forwarded directly to rental car companies.

⛽ Gas Stations

Distances between stations can increase on intercity roads. Check your fuel tank before driving up mountain roads.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cyprus

All the details and frequently asked questions you need to know before exploring the island with a rental car.

Since Cyprus was under British rule for many years, the traffic system flows on the left, just like in the UK. You might find it a bit challenging to adapt to the steering wheel and indicator stalks being on the opposite side during the first 15-20 minutes; however, your reflexes will adapt quickly thanks to the warning signs on the roads and the flow of other traffic. It is particularly important to remember that when entering roundabouts, you must always yield to vehicles coming from the right.

Yes, your Republic of Turkey (TR) driving license is fully valid for up to 3 months from the moment you enter Cyprus as a tourist. There is no need to obtain an extra international driving license or translate your license. At police checkpoints, showing your driving license along with the white "entry document" (or passport) handed to you when entering the island is sufficient.

Crossing to South Cyprus is strictly prohibited with the red-plated cars you rent in North Cyprus. Rental company insurances do not cover the other side of the border. If you wish to cross to South Cyprus, you must cross on foot through the border checkpoints or rent a separate vehicle from rental companies located on the other side.

There are "Fixed Speed Cameras" on almost every main road and city center in Cyprus, and they operate very precisely. The speed limit inside cities is generally 50 km/h, while on double-lane intercity highways, it is 65, 80, or a maximum of 100 km/h. Exceeding the speed limit by even 1 km/h will trigger a fine. For rental cars, these fines are directly sent to the rental company and deducted from your deposit, or they will appear at the airport when you are leaving the island.

Google Maps is quite adequate for finding main roads throughout Cyprus; however, it can sometimes direct you to very narrow city streets or dirt roads. The most up-to-date and popular navigation alternative on the island is Yandex Navigation. To guard against running out of internet data, we recommend downloading the Cyprus map offline to your phone before setting off.

Your carrier plans from Turkey are not valid in North Cyprus, and you may face high roaming charges if you turn on your cellular data. The most practical and economical solution is to purchase a digital eSIM. To use the ready map routes and navigation seamlessly, you can select a "Cyprus" package from global apps like Airalo or Holafly before your trip and set it up on your phone in seconds. This way, your internet becomes active as soon as you land, without the need to search for a physical SIM card. Alternatively, you can buy physical tourist data SIMs from local carriers (North Cyprus Turkcell or Telsim) inside Ercan Airport.
